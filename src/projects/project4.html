Facilitating Creativity through Large language Model-enabled Social Robots
(In Progress)

This project is inspired by findings from my previous research, where it was observed that social robots had a positive and significant impact on language subjects. Intrigued by these results, this study aims to further investigate whether social robots powered by large language models can facilitate students' creativity in a writing workshop.

This is our lab's member, named "Xiaobao".

Currently recruiting participants until the end of Novermber.

1. The study are structured as a workshop, bringing together three groups of students for a writing challenge . The first group, serving as the control group, will create their poetry with the assistance of a tablet, with which they are free to search anything online. The second group will interact with a social robot powered by ChatGPT, while the third group will use a robot powered by a language model specifically trained on creative writing.

2. To ensure a thorough and objective evaluation, a mixed-method approach will be adopted. First, professional language teachers and experienced students will grade each poem on the basis of orginality and the richness of their poetic language. Second, the total creative time of each student will be considered. This includes the time spent brainstorming, writing, revising, and interacting with tablets/robots. These data will be coded and analyzed to discern any statistical significance in the results among the three groups. Qualitative data will be collected through interviews with selected students who interacted with social robots. By integrating both quantitative and qualitative findings, this study aims to shed light on the potential of social robots as a learning companion for fostering creativity in language.
